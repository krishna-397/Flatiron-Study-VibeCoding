SPATIAL CANVASES

the flatiron at four scales — layout as computation

← the flatiron plate  ·  relational structures  ·  geospatial structures →

The Flatiron Building, subject of my precedent plate on this site, is the hinge of the argument: it exists because Broadway's diagonal left an unbuildable sliver in the grid, and the building is the sliver's answer. Here it returns at every scale — as a navigable massing, in its neighborhood under a moving sun, generated by the grid, and standing on ground that was farmland a century before. My intention is to carry this subject through each of the course's digital objects — spatial, temporal, relational, geo-spatial, engagement, agents — as an ongoing method for analyzing buildings, objects, and spaces in computational terms.

PLATE 01 · CONTEXT & SUN

The Flatiron within its blocks, set on a circular base like a model on a plinth — a true axonometric (orthographic camera, no perspective). The arc overhead is the sun's path: drag the slider from morning to evening and watch the tower's shadow sweep its neighborhood. In 1902 nothing around it stood close to its height — the shadow had the streets to itself.

PLATE 03 · WHEN MANHATTAN WAS FARMS

The same ground, a century earlier: long farm lots running skew to the island, drawn from the old farms-and-estates surveys. Bloomingdale Road — the future Broadway — winds through them, and the modern grid is ghosted on top in dashed ink. The solid wedge marks where the road and the grid will one day argue, and the Flatiron will be the verdict. Static plate, drawn once from a fixed seed; hatches and light pastel washes over ink.

static survey · farm lots c. 1800, grid of 1811 ghosted above

PLATE 04 · THE GRID AND THE WEDGE

Situating the building as it sits in reality: north is up, the grid leans 29 degrees east of north, and Broadway runs nearly north–south through it. Wherever the diagonal crosses a block, sliver parcels appear — drawn solid. Move your mouse to swing Broadway and watch the grid manufacture flatirons; the labeled wedge at 23rd and Fifth is the one that got a building.
